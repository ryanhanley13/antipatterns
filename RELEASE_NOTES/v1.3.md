The catalog gets its first research refresh and the scanner gets meaningfully sharper at catching what models actually write. If you ran v1.2, update to pick up the new patterns and the inflection-aware detection.

## What's new

- **17 new catalogued patterns**, research-driven from 2024–2026 excess-word studies (arXiv, FSU, Oxford). The big one: `underscore` and the phrase "underscores the importance of," which were missing entirely. Plus `intricate`, `meticulous`, `comprehensive`, `adept`, `vibrant`, `bustling`, `ever-evolving`, `beacon`, `showcase`, `Notably,`, and stock phrases ("a testament to," "plays a pivotal/key role," "sheds light on," "rich tapestry"). All Tier 2 (density) except the stock phrases (Tier 1).
- **Inflection-aware scanning.** `scan.py` now counts inflected forms, not just exact words — so `leveraged`, `navigating`, `fostering`, `seamlessly`, `holistically` register. Before, a draft full of "leveraging" and "navigating" scanned clean. Carefully bounded: Tier 3 stays exact, and a word that's also a separate catalog entry never double-counts.
- **Whole-piece density ceiling.** A new `DENSE` flag catches AI-density spread across an entire piece (≥5 Tier-2 hits per 1000 words over 200+ words) — the diffuse case paragraph clusters structurally can't see.
- **Adverb families and one irregular** now match too: `holistically` / `strategically` / `sustainably` (the `-ically` / `-ably` forms), and `drove`.

## For contributors

- `add_pattern.py` is safer: the tiered-section writer no longer orphans indented children, and the error for a new section names the exact manual steps (new sections stay manual by design — `SECTIONS` in `catalog.py` is the single source of truth).
- Catalog: 183 → 200 lexical entries; test suite 59 → 71. Two real bugs caught on review by Codex (a double-count, and an adverb-spelling gap) and fixed with regression tests.
