The scanner can now help *grow* the catalog, not just check it. New `--discover` mode lists uncataloged words that repeat in your draft — the freshest signal for what AI tells are slipping through, mined from your own writing.

## What's new

- **`scan.py --discover`** — candidate discovery. Run it on a draft and it lists words that repeat (default ≥3×) but aren't already in the catalog, as removal-test candidates:

  ```
  $ scan.py draft.md --discover
  ...
  CANDIDATES (uncataloged repeats - removal test / consider adding)
    synergize x4, operationalize x3
  ```

  Those feed straight into `add_pattern.py --apply` — so the catalog grows itself from your actual drafts instead of waiting on a periodic research pass.

- **Stays honest:** excludes everything the scanner already catches (catalog entries + their inflections — so `leveraged` won't re-suggest `leverage`), filters a stoplist + short tokens so common words don't dominate, and is advisory only. Candidates are never flags, `--strict` is unaffected, and `--discover-min N` tunes the threshold.

No catalog or detection changes since v1.3 — this is a tooling release. (The catalog research refresh, inflection matching, and density ceiling all shipped in v1.3.)
